Warm Autumn is distinctly warm and golden. Your colours are lively, approachable, and naturally radiant. If it looks too muted, greyed out, cool, or too dark and heavy, it has gone too far toward Autumn territory. If it looks warm, golden, and naturally alive, you are in the right place.

This is inspiration, not rules. Every colour in your palette works with you and with each other. Use this guide as a reference and a starting point as you learn your colours. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction. Take what feels right, when it feels right, and leave what doesn't.

The Warm Autumn palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches here are carefully chosen examples to help you understand the qualities to look for, warm, golden, and naturally radiant. When you shop, use these as a visual compass. Warm Autumn flows toward Spring, which means your colours can have a liveliness that True Autumn does not need. Keep them warm and golden. If a colour starts to feel cool, pastel, or overly bright and clear, it has gone too far.

Your essence: Spectacular. Lively. Distinctive. Natural. Approachable. Friendly. These words describe the energy your palette carries. This guide gives you permission to lean into your warmth fully and wear it with joy.

A note on blues: Blue is the most challenging colour family for Warm Autumn. All the blues in this palette sit on the warmer, teal-leaning side of the hue scale rather than a pure cool blue. Even so, they can be difficult to render accurately on screen. In real life, look for blues that feel warm or golden. Blues work beautifully in patterns, as a trouser or jacket, or mixed with your warmer tones. When in doubt, lean toward the teals and aquas which are more confidently yours.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display.

Makeup & Hair
Your Warm Autumn guide to beauty. Warm, golden, and naturally radiant. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.

Your golden rule: If it looks cool, icy, or pastel, put it back. Warm Autumn makeup is warm, deep, and intentional. A rich warm garnet or warm garnet lip with minimal eye makeup will always look more "you" than a full cool face. Your depth and warmth do the work, let your colours support that.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation consideration: warm or golden undertone. Warm and golden-based foundations work best. Pink-based or cool foundations will look ashy and fight your warm colouring.
What to look for
Ask for warm or golden undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as pink, cool, or neutral-cool. Warm Autumn spans a range of skin tones, so your shade depth will vary, but the undertone quality stays the same. Tip: when testing foundation, a warm match that looks alive and naturally golden is right. One that looks ashy, grey, or pink-toned is too cool for you.
Eyes
Your eyes thrive with warm depth and rich definition. Bronze, copper, warm gold, and forest greens are deeply flattering. The warm brown and golden palette was made for you.
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: warm ivory, golden beige, light caramel, warm peach. Depth: warm brown, golden brown, medium chestnut, warm toffee. Accents: bronze, copper, warm gold, golden teal, burnt sienna. Rich metallic and satin finishes look spectacular on Warm Autumn. Avoid: cool grey, silver, icy lavender, stark black, or any cool metallic.
Eyeliner
Warm dark brown or espresso is your most flattering liner. If you want more drama, try dark chestnut, deep chocolate, or deep forest green. Avoid cool grey or blue-black liner, which creates a harsh coolness that fights your warm-neutral depth. Tip: a deep warm brown liner with a brick red lip is a quintessential Warm Autumn look.
Mascara
Warm brown or dark espresso mascara is your most flattering choice. dark chestnut works beautifully too. Pure cool black can work but may feel stark, if you use it, pair with softer warm eye makeup elsewhere. Try: any brown-black shade for a rich, natural finish that enhances rather than harshens.
Brows & Beard
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade warmer and slightly deeper. A warm dark brown or dark chestnut brow product works beautifully. Avoid anything with ash, cool, or grey undertones. Tip: ask for "warm brown" or "dark warm brown" when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Beard & grooming colour
Facial hair looks excellent in this season, the natural depth and warmth of Warm Autumn colouring suits well-kept facial hair beautifully. If you colour your beard or brows, look for deep warm brown, espresso, or dark chestnut shades. Avoid anything with ash, cool, or blue-black tones. Tip: keep lines clean and intentional, Warm Autumn suits structured grooming that feels deliberate.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. Warm Autumn's natural colouring tends toward warm, golden, medium-toned hair, and that is exactly what flatters you most.
Your best hair colours
Deep and warm: deep brown, espresso, dark chestnut, and auburn-brown are all stunning. These shades echo your natural depth and colouring. Avoid: ashy tones, platinum or icy highlights, cool black, or anything that introduces coolness or lightness to your hair, these work against your natural richness.
Highlights & colour techniques
If you highlight, choose deep warm caramel, auburn, or copper tones only. Keep them subtle and close in depth to your base, Warm Autumn does not suit high-contrast highlighting. Avoid: cool ash highlights, platinum balayage, or any technique that introduces icy or light tones. Depth and warmth are your strengths.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "warm toned," "no ash," and "deep" for your shade. Show your colourist your palette, having your actual colour reference makes the conversation much easier. Tip: when in doubt, go deeper and warmer rather than lighter or cooler.
Shopping tip: When you find a product you love but are not sure if it works, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand Name] [Product Name] in [Shade Name] warm or cool toned? Is it suitable for Warm Autumn?" Then do a visual check by comparing the product swatch against your palette. Trust your eye.
